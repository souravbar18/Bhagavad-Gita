Chapter 17, Shloka 17

श्रद्धया परया तप्तं तपस्तत्त्रिविधं नरैः।
अफलाकाङ्क्षिभिर्युक्तैः सात्त्विकं परिचक्षते।। १७.१७ ।।

Summary Translation:

This threefold austerity, performed with supreme faith by men who do not expect material benefits but are engaged only for the sake of the Supreme, is called austerity in goodness.

Detailed Analysis:

In this verse, Lord Krishna defines the Application of Sattvic austerity (Tapas). He explains that the Threefold austerity (of body, speech, and mind) becomes Sattvic when it is performed with Shraddhaya Paraya—supreme faith. This Analysis is Critical because it shows that Action without Faith is Incomplete. Krishna emphasizes that the Performer must be Aphalakankshibhir (without desire for fruit) and Yuktaih (engaged in yoga). This Framework proves that Goodness is Defined by its Motivation rather than just its Form. For a Hindu, this Shloka is a Manual for Purity. Krishna is Identifying that Sattva requires Transcendence of material Rewards. The analysis reveals that Sattvic austerity is the Path to Liberation. Krishna’s Diagnosis proves that Faith (Shraddha) is the Engine of spiritual Progress. The Significance of this shloka is that it provides a Standard for Tapasya. Krishna’s Insight proves that Discipline is Empowered by Devotion. The Lesson here is that Excellence is Possible only when Egotism is Removed. This verse acts as a Guide for Sincerity, confirming that Success is the Result of Alignment with the Divine. Ultimately, Salvation is Accessible to those who Practice with Detachment. This shloka is a Celebration of Integrity in the Spiritual life.
