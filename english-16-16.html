Chapter 16, Shloka 16

अनेकचित्तविभ्रान्ता मोहजालसमावृताः।
प्रसक्ताः कामभोगेषु पतन्ति नरकेऽशुचौ।। १६.१६ ।।

Summary Translation:

Thus perplexed by various anxieties and bound by a network of illusions, they become too strongly attached to sense enjoyment and fall down into hell.

Detailed Analysis:

In this verse, Lord Krishna explains the Climax of the demonic mindset, leading to their ultimate Downfall. The phrase Aneka-chitta-vibhrantah indicates that their minds are scattered by Confusion and countless Distractions. This state of being perplexed by many thoughts is a Symptom of a soul that has disconnected from the Singularity of the Supreme Lord. Krishna describes them as being Moha-jala-samavritah—enmeshed in the network of Illusion. This Metaphor of a net suggests that Maya traps the demonic soul so tightly that Liberation becomes impossible. The Obsession with Kama-bhogesu (sense gratification) acts as the Weight that drags them down. The analysis reveals that the demonic are Addicted to the Pleasure of the material world. For a Hindu, the ultimate Consequence is Patanti Narake-ashuchau—falling into a foul hell. This is not just a Punishment, but a Destination earned through Uncleanliness (ashuchau). Krishna is Identifying that the Path of the demonic is a Descent into darkness. The Significance of this shloka is that it shows the Correlation between Attachment and Bondage. When the Mind is filled with Greed, it loses its Intelligence. This Decay results in a Existence that is Void of peace. Krishna’s Diagnosis proves that the Network of desires is the Trap that keeps the soul away from Vaikuntha. The Gravity of their sins forces them into Abodes of suffering. Thus, this verse serves as a Warning for the seeker to Detach from Illusions. It reinforces the Truth that Sense Enjoyment is a Mirage that leads to Misery. The Foundation of a spiritual life must be Purity to avoid this Fate.
