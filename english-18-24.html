Chapter 18, Shloka 24

यत्तु कामेप्सुना कर्म साहङ्कारेण वा पुनः ।
क्रियते बहुलायासं तद्राजसमुदाहृतम् ॥ १८.२४ ॥

Summary Translation:

But action performed with great effort by one seeking to gratify his desires, and enacted from a sense of false ego, is called action in the mode of passion.

Detailed Analysis:

In this verse, Lord Krishna defines Rajasic Action. He describes the Characteristics of Work performed in the mode of Passion (Rajas). Such action is driven by kamepsuna—the Desire for Sense gratification—and is performed sahankarena—with a strong sense of False ego. Furthermore, it is marked by bahulayāsam, meaning it requires Extreme effort or Strenuous labor. This Analysis is Insightful because it identifies the Waste of Energy inherent in Selfish pursuits. For a Hindu, this Shloka is a Critique of Ambition and Greed. Krishna is Identifying that Passion leads to Exhaustion and Stress. The analysis reveals that Rajasic action Binds the Soul to the Cycle of Desire and Disappointment. Krishna’s Diagnosis proves that Success achieved through Egoism is Empty. The Significance of this shloka is that it provides a Warning against Over-exertion for Material gain. Krishna’s Insight proves that Peace is Sacrificed when Work is Motivated by Vanity. The Lesson here is that Growth is Thwarted by Self-Centeredness. This verse acts as a Guard against Burnout, confirming that Agitation is the Result of Passion. Ultimately, Salvation is Distant for those who Enslave themselves to their Cravings. This shloka is a Celebration of Simplicity and Humility. By Exposing the Burden of Rajasic action, Krishna Directs the Devotee toward Sattvic peace. It reinforces the Truth that the Divine is found in Calm effort rather than Frantic striving. Thus, Ambition becomes a Trap.
