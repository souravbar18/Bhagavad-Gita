Chapter 18, Shloka 20

सर्वभूतेषु येनैकं भावमव्ययमीक्षते ।
अविभक्तं विभक्तेषु तज्ज्ञानं विद्धि सात्त्विकम् ॥ १८.२० ॥

Summary Translation:

That knowledge by which one undivided spiritual nature is seen in all living entities, though they are divided into innumerable forms, you should understand to be in the mode of goodness.

Detailed Analysis:

In this verse, Lord Krishna defines Sattvic Knowledge. He explains that Knowledge is Sattvic when it enables one to Perceive ekam bhavam-avyayam—one imperishable Reality—in all Beings (sarva-bhuteshu). The Essential characteristic of this knowledge is the Ability to see the avibhaktam (undivided) within the vibhakteshu (divided/innumerable forms). This Analysis is Profound because it identifies Unity as the Highest form of Perception. For a Hindu, this Shloka is the Definition of Advaita or non-duality within Diversity. Krishna is Identifying that Goodness (Sattva) Transcends External differences like caste, species, or appearance. The analysis reveals that Sattvic knowledge Unifies the Soul with the Whole. Krishna’s Diagnosis proves that Hatred and Conflict are Impossible when this Knowledge is present. The Significance of this shloka is that it provides a Standard for Enlightenment. Krishna’s Insight proves that Truth is Consistent across all Life. The Lesson here is that Growth is Manifested in Compassion and Universalism. This verse acts as a Beacon for Living, confirming that Liberation is the Fruit of Oneness. Ultimately, Salvation is Realized by those who See the Divine in Every heart. This shloka is a Celebration of Love and Vision. By Describing the Sattvic mind, Krishna Inspires the Devotee to seek the Infinite in the Finite. It reinforces the Truth that the Divine is Indivisible despite Multiplicity. Thus, Perception becomes a Act of worship.
