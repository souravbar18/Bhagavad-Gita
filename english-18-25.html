Chapter 18, Shloka 25

अनुबन्धं क्षयं हिंसामनपेक्ष्य च पौरुषम् ।
मोहादारभ्यते कर्म यत्तत्तामसमुच्यते ॥ १८.२५ ॥

Summary Translation:

That action performed in illusion, in disregard of scriptural injunctions, and without concern for future bondage or for violence or distress caused to others, is said to be in the mode of ignorance.

Detailed Analysis:

In this verse, Lord Krishna defines Tamasic Action, the Lowest form of Conduct. He explains that such Work is initiated out of mohat (illusion or delusion) and Disregards four Factors: 1) Anubandham (future Consequences or Bondage), 2) Kshayam (the Loss of Wealth or Strength), 3) Himsam (the Injury or Violence caused to others), and 4) Paurusham (one's own Capacity or Ability to finish the task). This Analysis is Significant because it identifies the Recklessness inherent in Ignorance (Tamas). For a Hindu, this Shloka is a Warning against Irresponsibility. Krishna is Identifying that Tamas leads to Destruction and Sin. The analysis reveals that Tamasic action is Blind to the Reality of Cause and Effect. Krishna’s Diagnosis proves that Failure and Suffering are Guaranteed when Wisdom is Absent. The Significance of this shloka is that it provides a Map of Error. Krishna’s Insight proves that Action without Forethought is Poisonous. The Lesson here is that Growth is Obstructed by Mental darkness. This verse acts as a Guide for Discernment, confirming that Integrity is the Opposite of Delusion. Ultimately, Salvation is Impossible for those who Act with Malice or Folly. This shloka is a Celebration of Mindfulness and Responsibility. By Condemning this Reckless behavior, Krishna Encourages the Devotee to act with Clarity and Compassion. It reinforces the Truth that the Divine demands Awareness of our Impact on the world. Thus, Recklessness becomes a Crime.
