Chapter 18, Shloka 17

यस्य नाहङ्कृतो भावो बुद्धिर्यस्य न लिप्यते ।
हत्वापि स इमाँल्लोकान्न हन्ति न निबध्यते ॥ १८.१७ ॥

Summary Translation:

One who is not motivated by false ego, whose intelligence is not entangled, though he kills men in this world, he does not kill, nor is he bound by his actions.

Detailed Analysis:

In this verse, Lord Krishna provides the Resolution to Arjuna’s Crisis. He describes the State of a person who has Transcended the Ego. Such an individual has nahankrito bhavo—no sense of being the Doer—and their buddhi (intelligence) is na lipyate—not Stained or entangled by Actions. Krishna makes the Provocative statement that even if such a person were to Kill in the line of duty, they do not Kill and are not Bound by the result. This Analysis is Subliminal because it focuses on the Purity of the Internal state over the External act. For a Hindu, this Shloka is a Revelation of the Mechanics of Freedom. Krishna is Identifying that Bondage is a Product of Attachment to Doership. The analysis reveals that when the Ego is Removed, the Action becomes Transparent. Krishna’s Diagnosis proves that Guilt and Karma are Bound to the Ego. The Significance of this shloka is that it provides a Absolution for Duties performed in Surrender. Krishna’s Insight proves that Life and Death are Manifested through the Divine, and the Instrument is Blameless. The Lesson here is that Excellence is Found in Selflessness. This verse acts as a Guide for Warfare and Life, confirming that Peace is the Reward of Detachment. Ultimately, Salvation is Inherent in the State of no-ego. This shloka is a Celebration of Transcendence. By Illustrating the Paradox of Actionless Action, Krishna Resolves Arjuna’s Fear of Sin. It reinforces the Truth that the Divine is the Ultimate doer. Thus, Surrender becomes a Liberation.
