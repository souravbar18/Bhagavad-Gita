Chapter 18, Shloka 34

यया तु धर्मकामार्थान्धृत्या धारयतेऽर्जुन ।
प्रसङ्गेन फलाकांक्षी धृति: सा पार्थ राजसी ॥ १८.३४ ॥

Summary Translation:

But that determination by which one holds fast to fruitive results in religion, economic development and sense gratification is of the nature of passion, O Arjuna.

Detailed Analysis:

In this verse, Lord Krishna defines Rajasic Dhriti. He describes the Drive that is Rooted in Passion (Rajas). This Determination is used to dharayate (hold fast/pursue) the three Goals of Material life: Dharma (here meaning Ritualistic piety for gain), Kama (sense gratification), and Artha (economic development). Krishna highlights that this pursuit is characterized by being phalakankshi—being Obsessed with the Fruit or Results. This Analysis is Insightful because it identifies the Source of Ambition and Restlessness. For a Hindu, this Shloka is a Critique of Wordly success without Spiritual depth. Krishna is Identifying that Rajas makes Determination a Tool for Greed. The analysis reveals that Rajasic determination is Attachment-based. Krishna’s Diagnosis proves that Stress and Agitation are the Inevitable companions of this Persistence. The Significance of this shloka is that it provides a Warning against Purpose that is Bound to Materialism. Krishna’s Insight proves that Determination can be Misplaced when it is Driven by Vanity. The Lesson here is that Growth is Stunted by Result-oriented striving. This verse acts as a Guard against Burnout, confirming that Passion is the Fuel for Attachment. Ultimately, Salvation is Obstructed by Rajasic Dhriti because it Chains the Soul to the Cycle of Want. This shloka is a Celebration of Detachment over Obsession. By Exposing the Narrowness of passionate determination, Krishna Encourages the Devotee to Shift their Focus toward Sattvic peace. It reinforces the Truth that the Divine is found in Duty without Greed. Thus, Ambition becomes a Weight.
