॥ Chapter 16, Shloka 5 ॥

दैवी सम्पद्विमोक्षाय निबन्धायासुरी मता।
मा शुचः सम्पदं दैवीमभिजातोऽसि पाण्डव।। १६.५ ।।

Summary Translation:

The transcendental qualities are conducive to liberation, whereas the demoniac qualities make for bondage. Do not worry, O son of Pāṇḍu, for you are born with the divine qualities.

Detailed Analysis:

In this crucial verse, Lord Krishna provides a Synthesis and a Promise. He declares that the Daivi Sampad (Divine Nature) is meant for Vimokshaya (liberation), whereas the Asuri Sampad (Demonic Nature) leads to Nibandhaya (bondage). This Conclusion reveals that Character is the Architect of one's destiny. Krishna then consoles Arjuna with the words ma shuchah—do not grieve. He Assures Arjuna that he is born of a Divine Nature (sampadam daivim abhijato 'si). This analysis highlights that Fear and Doubt are removed by the Lord's Word. For a Hindu, this shloka is a Anchor, confirming that Virtue results in Grace. Krishna is Instructing Arjuna that his Duty (Dharma) as a warrior is Compatible with his divine nature. The Significance of this verse is the Distinction between the soul's Freedom and its Slavery to the Gunas. This Detailed Analysis underscores that Arjuna is Qualified for the Highest Good. The Lord's Assurance proves that Sincerity is Recognized by the Supreme Person. Ultimately, this verse Seals the Fate of the seeker, identifying Liberation as the Reward for cultivating divine qualities. This exhaustive analysis ensures that the Mind understands the Gravity of character choice. By Affirming Arjuna's status, Krishna Empowers him to act without Hesitation, knowing that his Essence is pure. This verse provides the Courage needed to Engage in the battle of life with a Certainty of success and eternal Liberation. It establishes that Liberation is Inherent in those who Cultivate the divine qualities, paving the Path to Eternity through Virtues.
