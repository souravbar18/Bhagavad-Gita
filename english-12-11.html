॥ Chapter 12, Shloka 11 ॥

अथैतदप्यशक्तोऽसि कर्तुं मद्योगमाश्रित: |
सर्वकर्मफलत्यागं तत: कुरु यतात्मवान् || १२.११ ||

Summary Translation:

If, however, you are unable to work in this consciousness, then try to act giving up all results of your work and try to be self-situated.

Detailed Analysis:

Krishna identifies the 'Theoretic Foundation for the Common Man.' He addresses the seeker who is 'athaitad apy aśhakto ’si' (even unable to do the previous step of working directly for Him). This analysis focuses on the 'Theoretic Strategy' of 'sarva-karma-phala-tyāgaṁ' (renouncing the fruits of all activities). This detailed analysis reveals that Krishna defines this as the ' Entry Point' for those who cannot yet dedicate their work specifically to a Divine Cause. This shloka provides a ' Framework for Detachment.' It teaches that if ' Devotion' (doing it for Krishna) is too difficult, one should at least practice ' Neutrality' toward the results. Krishna is telling Arjuna that 'By becoming 'yatātmavān' (self-controlled) and letting go of ' Ownership' over success and failure, you clear the ' Obstacles' of the ego.' This is the ' Goal of the Detached Seeker,' where 'The Renunciation' is 'Localized'.

The detailed analysis of 'Sarva-karma-phala-tyāgaṁ' (Renouncing fruits of all work) is the 'Theoretic Description of Spiritual De-cluttering.' In our practical lives, we are 'Result-oriented.' Krishna is defining 'The Self' as the 'Neutral Observer' who waits for the devotee to stop being anxious about outcomes. This shloka provides a 'Framework for the Beginner's Path.' By focusing on 'Tyāga' (giving up), Krishna is showing that ' Progress' can start with 'Subtraction'—subtracting the greed for results. Arjuna is defining 'Yatātmavān' as the ' Discipline' of the self. This verse is the ' Proof that the spiritual path is inclusive of all levels of capability.' By offering this lowest rung, he covers the 'Universal Accessibility' of the Gita's wisdom. This shloka is the 'Universal Manual for Reducing Stress through Faith,' forcing us to realize that 'We are responsible for the effort, but God is the master of the result'. This is the ' Goal of the Surrendered Worker,' where 'The Peace' is 'Localized'.
