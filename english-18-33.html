Chapter 18, Shloka 33

धृत्या यया धारयते मन:प्राणेन्द्रियक्रिया: ।
योगेनाव्यभिचारिण्या धृति: सा पार्थ सात्त्विकी ॥ १८.३३ ॥

Summary Translation:

O son of Prtha, that determination which is unbreakable, which is sustained with steadfastness by yoga practice, and thus controls the mind, life and the acts of the senses, is in the mode of goodness.

Detailed Analysis:

In this verse, Lord Krishna defines Sattvic Dhriti (determination/fortitude). He explains that Sattvic determination is avyabhicharinya—unwavering and Focused—and is Developed through Yoga (spiritual discipline). This Force is used to dharayate (sustain/control) the Functions of the manah (mind), prana (life-breath/energy), and indriya-kriyah (sensory actions). This Analysis is Empowering because it identifies Self-Control as the Pinnacle of Fortitude. For a Hindu, this Shloka is the Manual for Willpower. Krishna is Identifying that Sattva provides the Strength to Subjugate the Lower impulses. The analysis reveals that Sattvic determination is Goal-oriented toward the Absolute. Krishna’s Diagnosis proves that Mental stability is Achieved through Yogic practice. The Significance of this shloka is that it provides a Link between Discipline and Freedom. Krishna’s Insight proves that Determination without Purity is Fragile. The Lesson here is that Growth is Sustained by Sattvic Dhriti. This verse acts as a Guide for Mastery over the Self, confirming that Resilience is the Fruit of Yoga. Ultimately, Salvation is Realized by those who Harness their Energies for Evolution. This shloka is a Celebration of Power and Poise. By Defining the Noble determination, Krishna Empowers the Devotee to Integrate their Senses and Mind for the Supreme purpose. It reinforces the Truth that the Divine is the Strength of the Steadfast soul. Thus, Self-Governance becomes a Victory.
