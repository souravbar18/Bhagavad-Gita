Chapter 16 , Shloka 4

यजन्ते सात्त्विका देवान्यक्षरक्षांसि राजसाः।
प्रेतान्भूतगणांश्चान्ये यजन्ते तामसा जनाः।। १७.४ ।।

Summary Translation:

Men in the mode of goodness worship the demigods; those in the mode of passion worship the demons; and those in the mode of ignorance worship ghosts and spirits.

Detailed Analysis:

In this shloka, Lord Krishna provides a Application of the theory of modes by describing the Objects of worship chosen by different types of people. This Breakdown is Profound because it shows how one’s internal Mode dictates their external Affiliation. Those in Goodness (Sattvika) worship the Devas (demigods), who are Empowered by the Supreme to manage the universe. This Worship is Organized and leads to Peace. Those in Passion (Rajasa) worship Yaksha-rakshamsi (demons or powerful beings), seeking Wealth and Power. Their Motivation is purely Selfish. Finally, those in Ignorance (Tamasa) worship Pretan (ghosts) and Bhuta-ganan (spirits). This Worship is Degraded and rooted in Fear and Delusion. Krishna is Identifying that the Destination of a soul is tied to the Object of its worship. For a Hindu, this is a Warning to not be Misled by lower forms of devotion. Krishna’s Insight proves that Faith is always Directed toward something. The Significance of this shloka is that it provides a Test for evaluating one’s own spiritual Orientation. If a person is Attracted to Darkness, their mode is Tamas. If they are Driven by Ambition, it is Rajas. Krishna’s Diagnosis proves that true Spirituality (Bhakti) must Transcend all these material modes. The Lesson here is that Purity in worship is Essential for reaching the Supreme Lord. This verse acts as a Map for Discernment. It reinforces the Truth that the Quality of our Association shapes our Future. Ultimately, Salvation is Reserved for those who Worship the Supreme Lord with Sincerity beyond the modes. This shloka is a Call to Higher Consciousness.
