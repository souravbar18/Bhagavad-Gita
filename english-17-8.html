Chapter 17, Shloka 8

आयुःसत्त्वबलारोग्यसुखप्रीतिविवर्धनाः।
रस्याः स्निग्धाः स्थिरा हृद्या आहाराः सात्त्विकप्रियाः।। १७.८ ।।

Summary Translation:

Foods dear to those in the mode of goodness increase the duration of life, purify one’s existence and give strength, health, happiness and satisfaction. Such foods are juicy, fatty, wholesome, and pleasing to the heart.

Detailed Analysis:

In this verse, Lord Krishna defines the Characteristics of food in the Mode of goodness (Sattva). He states that such Foods are Ayuh-sattva-balarogya—increasing life, purity, and strength. This Analysis establishes a Connection between Nutrition and Consciousness. For a Hindu, food in the mode of goodness is Sacred because it supports Spiritual Practice. Krishna describes these foods as Rasyah (juicy), Snigdhah (fatty or natural oils), Sthirah (wholesome/sustaining), and Hridyah (pleasing to the heart). This Profile of Sattvic food is Balanced, providing Nourishment without causing Agitation. Krishna is Identifying that those who are Sattvika-priyah (lovers of goodness) naturally gravitate toward Purity in their diet. The analysis reveals that Sattvic food is the Foundation for a Long Life and Health. Krishna’s Diagnosis proves that Happiness (Sukha) and Satisfaction (Priti) are Enhanced by the right kind of food. The Significance of this shloka is that it provides a Standard for Living. By Choosing these foods, the soul can Cultivate a state of Peace. Krishna’s Insight proves that Food is not just for the Body, but for the Mind. The Lesson here is that Evolution is Aided by Sattvic intake. This verse acts as a Guide for Health, confirming that Well-being is the Result of Alignment with nature's modes. Ultimately, Salvation is Supported by a Body that is Pure. This shloka is a Celebration of the Vitality that comes from Goodness.
