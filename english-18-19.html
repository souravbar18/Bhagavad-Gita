Chapter 18, Shloka 19

ज्ञानं कर्म च कर्ता च त्रिधैव गुणभेदतः ।
प्रोच्यते गुणसङ्ख्याने यथावच्छृणु तान्यपि ॥ १८.१९ ॥

Summary Translation:

According to the three different modes of material nature, there are three kinds of knowledge, action and performer of action. Now hear of them from Me.

Detailed Analysis:

In this verse, Lord Krishna introduces the Application of the Three Gunas (modes of nature) to the Triad of Jnanam (knowledge), Karma (action), and Karta (the doer). He explains that according to Guna-sankhyane (the science of the modes), these three are Classified into three types each. Krishna invites Arjuna to yathavach-chhrinu—hear them exactly as they are. This Analysis is Systematic because it provides a Framework for Spiritual auditing. For a Hindu, this Shloka is the Key to Self-Assessment. Krishna is Identifying that Nature (Prakriti) Colors every Aspect of our Spiritual life. The analysis reveals that Knowledge can be Divided based on its Vibration. Krishna’s Diagnosis proves that Freedom is Possible only when we Move toward Sattva (goodness). The Significance of this shloka is that it provides a Standard for Excellence. Krishna’s Insight proves that Growth is Measured by the Shift in our Gunas. The Lesson here is that Transformation is Systematic. This verse acts as a Bridge to the Detailed descriptions that follow. Krishna’s Authority confirms that Knowledge is the Foundation of Change. Ultimately, Salvation is Facilitated by those who Cultivate Sattvic traits. This shloka is a Celebration of Diversity within Unity. By Promising to Explain these Categories, Krishna Teaches the Devotee that Spirituality is not Vague but Precise. It reinforces the Truth that the Divine is the Source of all Wisdom. Thus, Study of the Gunas becomes a Duty.
