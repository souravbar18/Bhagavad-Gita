Chapter 17, Shloka 18

सत्कारमानपूजार्थं तपो दम्भेन चैव यत्।
क्रियते तदिह प्रोक्तं राजसं चलमध्रुवम्।। १७.१८ ।।

Summary Translation:

Penance performed out of pride and for the sake of gaining respect, honor and worship is said to be in the mode of passion. It is neither stable nor permanent.

Detailed Analysis:

In this verse, Lord Krishna defines Rajasic austerity (Tapas). He explains that when Discipline is performed Satkara-mana-pujartham—for the sake of Respect, honor, and worship—it is Passionate. This Analysis reveals that Rajas is Rooted in Pride (Dambha). Krishna describes such Action as Chalam (unstable) and Adhruvam (non-permanent). This Framework proves that Passion leads to Frustration because the Rewards it seeks are fleeting. For a Hindu, this Shloka is a Warning against Ostentation in Spiritual life. Krishna is Identifying that Passion turns Service into a Performance. The analysis reveals that Rajasic austerity is the Barrier to Peace. Krishna’s Diagnosis proves that Greed for Social standing Corrupts the soul. The Significance of this shloka is that it provides a Mirror for Ambition. Krishna’s Insight proves that Stability (Dhruvam) is Impossible in Rajas. The Lesson here is that Value is Diminished when Ego is the Driver. This verse acts as a Caution against Hypocrisy, confirming that Failure is the Outcome of Vanity. Ultimately, Salvation is Hindered by Rajasic attachments. This shloka is a Call to Humility over Display. By Understanding the nature of Rajas, a person can Avoid the Trap of fleeting Success.
