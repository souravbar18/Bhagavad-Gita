Chapter 17, Shloka 22

अदेशकाले यद्दानमपात्रेभ्यश्च दीयते।
असत्कृतमवज्ञातं तत्तामसमुदाहृतम्।। १७.२२ ।।

Summary Translation:

And charity performed at an improper place and time and given to unworthy persons without respect and with contempt is called charity in the mode of ignorance.

Detailed Analysis:

In this verse, Lord Krishna defines Tamasic charity (Dana) by highlighting its Negligence. He mentions that Giving becomes Ignorant when it happens at Adesha-kale—improper place and time—and is given to Apatrebhyah—unworthy recipients. Furthermore, it is described as Asatkritam (without respect) and Avajnatam (with contempt). This Analysis is Severe because it reveals that Tamas is not just about Inaction but about Disrespectful action. For a Hindu, this Shloka is a Warning against Carelessness in Philanthropy. Krishna is Identifying that Tamas blinds the soul to the Value of the act. The analysis reveals that Tamasic charity is the Path to Degradation because it Insults both the giver and the receiver. Krishna’s Diagnosis proves that Contempt Negates the benefit of Charity. The Significance of this shloka is that it provides a Boundary for Generosity. Krishna’s Insight proves that Discernment is Mandatory. The Lesson here is that Growth is Thwarted when Giving is done Mechanically or with Anger. This verse acts as a Guide for Conduct, confirming that Failure is the Result of Ignorance. Ultimately, Salvation is Barred for those who Mock the Act of giving through Tamasic behavior. This shloka is a Call to Mindfulness and Respect in all Interactions. By Understanding the nature of Tamas, the seeker can Avoid the Sin of Contemptuous giving.
