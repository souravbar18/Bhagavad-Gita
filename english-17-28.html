Chapter 17, Shloka 28

अश्रद्धया हुतं दत्तं तपस्तप्तं कृतं च यत्।
असदित्युच्यते पार्थ न च तत्प्रेत्य नो इह।। १७.२८ ।।

Summary Translation:

Anything done as sacrifice, charity or penance without faith in the Supreme, O son of Pṛthā, is impermanent. It is called asat and is useless both in this life and the next.

Detailed Analysis:

In this concluding verse of Chapter 17, Lord Krishna delivers a Warning regarding the absolute necessity of Faith (Shraddha). He declares that Ashraddhaya—anything performed without faith—whether it be Hutam (sacrifice), Dattam (charity), or Taptam (austerity), is Defined as Asat (non-existent or false). This Analysis is Severe because it renders even the most elaborate Rituals completely Useless if the Engine of faith is missing. Krishna emphasizes that such Asat actions are Fruitless both Pretya (in the next life) and Iha (in this life). This Framework proves that Faith is the Currency of the spiritual world. For a Hindu, this Shloka is a Reminder that Sincerity outweighs Ceremony. Krishna is Identifying that Atheism or Doubt during spiritual acts Nullifies the merit of the deed. The analysis reveals that Asat is the Path to Waste. Krishna’s Diagnosis proves that Mechanization of religion without Heart is a Dead-end. The Significance of this shloka is that it provides a Conclusion to the discussion on the three modes of nature. Krishna’s Insight proves that Goodness (Sattva) must be Coupled with Devotion to reach the Supreme. The Lesson here is that Reality is Accessible only through Trust in the Divine. This verse acts as a Guard against Hypocrisy, confirming that Failure is the Outcome of Faithlessness. Ultimately, Salvation is Barred for those who Perform without Conviction. This shloka is a Call to Authenticity. By Cultivating Shraddha, the seeker Ensures that their Efforts are Recorded in the book of eternal Sat. It reinforces the Truth that the Internal state is the Arbiter of Value in the eyes of the Supreme Lord.
