॥ Chapter 14, Shloka 8 ॥

तमस्त्वज्ञानजं विद्धि मोहनं सर्वदेहिनाम्।
प्रमादालस्यनिद्राभिस्तन्निबध्नाति भारत।। १४.८ ।।

Summary Translation:

O son of Bharata, know that the mode of darkness, born of ignorance, is the delusion of all embodied beings. The results of this mode are madness, indolence and sleep, which bind the conditioned soul.

Detailed Analysis:

In this shloka, Lord Krishna provides a Anatomy of the mode of Tamas (ignorance). He describes Tamas as ajnana-jam—born of ignorance—and calls it mohanam sarva-dehinam, the Delusion of all living beings. This analysis presents Tamas as the Darkness that Obscures the truth. Unlike Rajas, which is hyperactive, or Sattva, which is clear, Tamas is Inert. Krishna explains that Tamas binds the soul through pramada (heedlessness/negligence), alasya (laziness), and nidra (sleep/stupor). This shloka provides a Explanation for why humans sometimes act against their own Interest. Tamas is the force that makes us Blind to the consequences of our actions. The analysis shows that Procrastination and Denial are the Chains of this mode. A person in Tamas lives in a Fog, unable to Discriminate between right and wrong. In a religious sense, this shloka provides the Warning against Spiritual Decay. If Rajas is a fire, Tamas is the Smoke that chokes the soul. The detailed interpretation highlights the Danger of pramada (heedlessness). It is the state where one knows what is right but Ignores it out of Indifference. Krishna addresses Arjuna as Bharata, Recalling his noble heritage, which is the Antidote to Tamasic laziness. This analysis helps a seeker to Diagnose their own Resistance to growth. One is encouraged to see their Lethargy not as Rest but as Bondage. This shloka provides the Logic for Discipline (Tapasya). it teaches that as long as we are Slothful, we are Trapped in the lowest level of nature. The religious significance is that Tamas Buries the soul under a Mountain of unconsciousness. It confirms that Comfort in ignorance is the Ultimate Failure. Thus, the detailed analysis of this verse points to the Necessity of Awakening (Jagrata) to dispel the Night of Tamas. It prepares the student to understand the Competition between the modes, after seeing the Stagnation of Tamas.
