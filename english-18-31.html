Chapter 18, Shloka 31

यया धर्ममधर्मं च कार्यं चाकार्यमेव च ।
अयथावत्प्रजानाति बुद्धिः सा पार्थ राजसी ॥ १८.३१ ॥

Summary Translation:

O son of Prtha, that understanding which cannot distinguish between religion and irreligion, between action that should be done and action that should not be done, is in the mode of passion.

Detailed Analysis:

In this verse, Lord Krishna defines Rajasic Buddhi. He explains that a Rajasic intellect ayathavat-prajanati—cannot Distinguish correctly—between Dharma (righteousness) and Adharma (unrighteousness), or between Karyam (what should be done) and Akaryam (what should not be done). This Analysis is Significant because it identifies Confusion as the Core of Passion (Rajas). For a Hindu, this Shloka is a Warning against Subjectivity and Ego-driven interpretation of Moral laws. Krishna is Identifying that Passion Distorts the Truth. The analysis reveals that a Rajasic mind is Clouded by Personal desires, leading to Ambiguity in Conduct. Krishna’s Diagnosis proves that Error is Inevitable when Intellect is Driven by Greed. The Significance of this shloka is that it provides a Critique of Relativism. Krishna’s Insight proves that Right and Wrong are Objective, but Passion makes them Hazy. The Lesson here is that Growth is Hindered by Partial or Biased understanding. This verse acts as a Guard against Hypocrisy, confirming that Agitation is the Result of Ignorance masquerading as Intelligence. Ultimately, Salvation is Obstructed by Rajasic Buddhi because it Fails to see the Boundary between Duty and Self-interest. This shloka is a Celebration of Truth over Distortion. By Describing the Confused intellect, Krishna Directs the Devotee to seek Sattvic clarity. It reinforces the Truth that the Divine demands Intellectual honesty. Thus, Misinterpretation of Dharma becomes a Trap.
