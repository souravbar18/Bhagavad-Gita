॥ Chapter 14, Shloka 17 ॥

सत्त्वात्सञ्जायते ज्ञानं रजसो लोभ एव च।
प्रमादमोहौ तमसो भवतोऽज्ञानमेव च।। १४.१७ ।।

Summary Translation:

From the mode of goodness, real knowledge develops; from the mode of passion, greed develops; and from the mode of ignorance, foolishness, madness and illusion develop.

Detailed Analysis:

In this verse, Lord Krishna provides a Lineage of mental states born from the three Gunas. He declares that from Sattva, Knowledge (jnanam) is born. From Rajas, Greed (lobhah) arises, and from Tamas, Heedlessness (pramadah), Delusion (mohah), and Ignorance (ajnanam) are produced. This analysis presents the Evolution of the human mind. It tells us that our Guna-profile determines our Mental Content. If one lives in Sattva, their Intellect is naturally Luminous, leading to the realization of the Divine. In a religious sense, this shloka explains the Origin of our internal struggles. Greed is not an external sin but a By-product of Rajas. Similarly, Delusion is the Shadow of Tamas. Krishna is Educating Arjuna that his Confusion (mohah) is a direct result of the Darkness of Tamas that has Overwhelmed his mind. This verse provides the Logic for Self-improvement. By Increasing Sattva through pure food, holy company, and meditation, one Invokes the birth of knowledge. The detailed interpretation emphasizes that Knowledge is the Child of purity. For a devotee, this is a Instruction to Focus on the Cause (the Guna) rather than the Symptom (the greed or delusion). This shloka thus serves as a Map of the Psyche, showing the Seeds of our wisdom and our folly.
