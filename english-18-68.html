Chapter 18, Shloka 68

य इदं परमं गुह्यं मद्भक्तेष्वभिधास्यति ।
भक्तिं मयि परां कृत्वा मामेवैष्यत्यसंशय: ॥ १८.६८ ॥

Summary Translation:

For one who explains this supreme secret to the devotees, pure devotional service is guaranteed, and at the end he will come back to Me.

Detailed Analysis:

In this verse, Lord Krishna glorifies the act of sharing the Gita's wisdom with others. He declares that whoever abhidhasyati (explains) this paramam guhyam (supreme secret) to His devotees is performing the highest service. Such a person will achieve bhaktim mayi param (supreme devotion unto Me) and mam evaishyati ashamshayah—will certainly come to Me without any doubt. This verse is the ultimate motivation for spiritual teachers and preachers in Hinduism. The analysis reveals that sharing wisdom is not just an academic act; it is a form of worship. Krishna is identifying that the highest way to please the Lord is to help His children find their way back to Him. This verse serves as a promise of liberation for those who dedicate their lives to spreading the message of the Gita. The phrase asamshayah (without doubt) underscores the absolute certainty of this result. Krishna's diagnosis proves that by giving spiritual light to others, our own darkness is automatically dispelled. This shloka provides a standard for a true spiritual leader. It encourages the individual to be an instrument for the Divine message. It reinforces the idea that the greatest gift one can give to another is the gift of knowledge (Vidya-dana). By teaching the Gita, a person is essentially participating in the Lord's own mission of Dharma-samsthapana (establishing righteousness). This service creates a deep bond between the teacher and the Lord. Thus, the Gita teaches that the best way to stabilize one's own faith is to share it with a heart full of love. Therefore, the act of Pravachan (preaching) or teaching the Gita is considered a high spiritual practice that leads directly to the eternal abode. It is a win-win situation where both the speaker and the listener are elevated. In summary, the Lord values those who act as messengers of His truth, promising them the highest state of devotion and ultimate union with Him.
