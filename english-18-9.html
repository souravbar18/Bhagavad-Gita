Chapter 18, Shloka 9

कार्यमित्येव यत्कर्म नियतं क्रियतेऽर्जुन ।
सङ्गं त्यक्त्वा फलञ्चैव स त्यागः सात्त्विको मतः ॥ १८.९ ॥

Summary Translation:

O Arjuna, when one performs his prescribed duty only because it ought to be done, and renounces all material association and all attachment to the fruit, his renunciation is said to be in the mode of goodness.

Detailed Analysis:

In this verse, Lord Krishna defines Sattvic Renunciation. He explains that Duty (Niyatam karma) becomes Perfect when it is performed with the Attitude of Karyam-ityeva—simply because it Ought to be done. The Condition is that the Individual must be Sangam tyaktva (without attachment) and Phalam tyaktva (without desire for results). This Analysis is Supreme because it provides the Solution to Binding. For a Hindu, this Shloka is the Blueprint for Spiritual excellence. Krishna is Identifying that Goodness (Sattva) lies in Obedience to Dharma. The analysis reveals that Sattvic renunciation is not about Giving Up of work, but the Giving Up of the Ego within the work. Krishna’s Diagnosis proves that Peace is Achieved when Duty is Sanctified by Selflessness. The Significance of this shloka is that it provides a Standard for Integrity. Krishna’s Insight proves that Action can be Liberating when Directed by Wisdom. The Lesson here is that Growth is Accelerated by Focus on Duty for its own sake. This verse acts as a Guide for Living, confirming that Liberation is the Reward of Sincerity. Ultimately, Salvation is Accessible to those who Practice Sattvic Tyaga in their Daily activities. This shloka is a Celebration of Purity. By Defining the highest form of renunciation, Krishna Empowers the Devotee to turn every Moment into a Prayer. It reinforces the Truth that the Divine resides in the Fulfilling of one's Purpose without Greed. Thus, Action becomes a Bridge to the Infinite.
