Chapter 18, Shloka 23

नियतं सङ्गरहितमरागद्वेषतः कृतम् ।
अफलप्रेप्सुना कर्म यत्तत्सात्त्विकमुच्यতে ॥ १८.२३ ॥

Summary Translation:

That action which is regulated and which is performed without attachment, without love or hatred, and without desire for entrepreneurial result, is said to be in the mode of goodness.

Detailed Analysis:

In this verse, Lord Krishna defines Sattvic Action. He outlines the Attributes of Work performed in the mode of Goodness. Such action must be niyatam (regulated/prescribed), sanga-rahitam (free from attachment), and araga-dveshatah kritam—performed without Liking (raga) or Disliking (dvesha). Furthermore, it must be done by someone who is aphala-prepsuna—one who does not Seek the Results. This Analysis is Perfect because it provides the Foundation for Karma Yoga. For a Hindu, this Shloka is the Benchmark for Professional and Personal conduct. Krishna is Identifying that Discipline and Emotional neutrality are the Keys to Purity. The analysis reveals that Sattvic action Elevates the Soul by Decoupling the Ego from the Deed. Krishna’s Diagnosis proves that Peace is Inherent in Duty when it is performed as an Offering. The Significance of this shloka is that it provides a Standard for Integrity. Krishna’s Insight proves that Action can be Sanctified when it is Aligned with Dharma. The Lesson here is that Growth is Guaranteed by Detachment. This verse acts as a Guide for Success, confirming that Freedom is the Result of Selflessness. Ultimately, Salvation is Accessible to those who Master their Intentions through Regulation. This shloka is a Celebration of Order and Balance. By Defining the highest form of action, Krishna Empowers the Devotee to turn every Obligation into a Ritual. It reinforces the Truth that the Divine is found in the Fulfilling of one's Purpose without Greed. Thus, Duty becomes a Path to the Absolute.
