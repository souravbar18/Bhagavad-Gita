Chapter 16 , Shloka 3

सत्त्वानुरूपा सर्वस्य श्रद्धा भवति भारत।
श्रद्धामयोऽयं पुरुषो यो यच्छ्रद्धः स एव सः।। १७.३ ।।

Summary Translation:

O son of Bharata, according to one’s existence under the various modes of nature, one evolves a particular kind of faith. The living being is said to be of a particular faith according to the modes he has acquired.

Detailed Analysis:

This shloka presents a Axiom of human identity: Shraddhamayo-'yam purusho—a person is what their Faith is. Krishna reveals that Faith is Sattvanurupa, meaning it is perfectly Aligned with one's Existence or internal character. This Analysis proves that Faith is not just something a person has, but something a person is. For a Hindu, this means that every Action, thought, and word is a Expression of their underlying faith. Krishna is Identifying that the Quality of one’s life is determined by the Mode (Sattva, Rajas, or Tamas) that dominates their heart. The analysis shows that if a person's Consciousness is purified, their Faith becomes Transcendent. However, as long as one is Conditioned, their faith will reflect their Bondage. The Significance of this shloka is that it provides a Definition of the self. Krishna’s Insight proves that Success or failure in spiritual life is Predicated on the nature of one's faith. This verse acts as a Mirror for the seeker, urging them to examine their Priorities. If a person Values worldly gain, their faith is Passionate. If they Seek truth, their faith is Good. Krishna’s Diagnosis proves that Transformation is only possible by Elevating the quality of one’s faith. The Lesson here is that Evolution requires the Cultivation of Sattva (goodness). This shloka is a Warning against Hypocrisy; one cannot Pretend to be spiritual if their Faith is rooted in Greed. Ultimately, Salvation is the result of Aligning one's Self with the Supreme. This verse reinforces the Truth that faith is the DNA of the soul's journey.
