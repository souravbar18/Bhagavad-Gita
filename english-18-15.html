Chapter 18, Shloka 15

शरीरवाङ्मनोभिर्यत्कर्म प्रारभते नरः ।
न्याय्यं वा विपरीतं वा पञ्चैते तस्य हेतवः ॥ १८.१५ ॥

Summary Translation:

Whatever right or wrong action a man performs by body, speech or mind is caused by these five factors.

Detailed Analysis:

In this verse, Lord Krishna provides a Generalization regarding the Application of the five factors of action. He states that whatever Work a human being (narah) performs through Sharira (body), Vak (speech), or Manas (mind), these five factors are the Causes (hetavah). Krishna emphasizes that this applies regardless of whether the Action is Nyayyam (just/right) or Vipritam (opposite/wrong). This Analysis is Impartial because it shows that the Laws of action are Universal. For a Hindu, this Shloka is a Explanation of the Mechanics of Karma. Krishna is Identifying that every Word, Thought, and Movement is Governed by the Interplay of the five factors. The analysis reveals that Responsibility is Distributed, even in the Commission of Sin. Krishna’s Diagnosis proves that Ignorance leads us to Claim Sovereignty over our Deeds. The Significance of this shloka is that it provides a Context for Morality. Krishna’s Insight proves that Righteousness is a Choice made within the Framework of the five causes. The Lesson here is that Awareness of these factors Purifies the Intention. This verse acts as a Guard against Arrogance, confirming that Wisdom is the Result of Discernment. Ultimately, Salvation is Facilitated by those who Utilize their Body, Speech, and Mind as Instruments of the Supreme. This shloka is a Celebration of Integrity. By Linking every Type of action to the Causes, Krishna Teaches the Devotee to be Mindful in every Moment. It reinforces the Truth that the Divine is present in the Functioning of the Universe. Thus, Existence becomes a Responsibility.
