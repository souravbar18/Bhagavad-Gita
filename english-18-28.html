Chapter 18, Shloka 28

अयुक्तः प्राकृतः स्तब्धः शठो नैष्कृतिकोऽलसः ।
विषादी दीर्घसूत्री च कर्ता तामस उच्यते ॥ १८.२८ ॥

Summary Translation:

The worker who is always engaged in work against the injunction of the scripture, who is materialistic, obstinate, cheating and expert in insulting others, and who is lazy, always morose and procrastinating is said to be a worker in the mode of ignorance.

Detailed Analysis:

In this verse, Lord Krishna defines the Features of a Tamasic Karta or a doer in the mode of ignorance. This Analysis is Profound because it outlines the Degradation of human consciousness. The Tamasic doer is Ayuktah, meaning Uncontrolled and Disconnected from higher purpose. They are Prakritah, meaning Vulgar or Materialistic, lacking Refinement. Krishna uses the word Stabdha (obstinate) to describe their Rigidity. They are Shatha (cheating) and Naishkritikah (expert in Insulting others), reflecting a Lack of Integrity. Furthermore, they are Alasah (lazy), Vishadi (morose), and Dirghasutri (procrastinating). This Combination of Inertia and Malice proves that the Tamasic state is the Anthesis of spiritual progress. For a Hindu, this Shloka is a Warning against the Darkness of the mind. Krishna is Identifying that Laziness and Deceit are Poisons. The analysis reveals that a Tamasic person is Incapable of Achievement because they Waste time through Procrastination. Krishna’s Diagnosis proves that Moroseness is a Symptom of Ignorance. The Significance of this shloka is that it provides a Checklist for Self-Purification. Krishna’s Insight proves that Success is Obstructed by Obstinacy. The Lesson here is that Light is Blocked by Tamas. This verse acts as a Map of Failure. Ultimately, Salvation is Impossible for one who Dwells in Deceit and Sloth. This shloka is a Celebration of Honesty and Diligence. By Categorizing these Flaws, Krishna Empowers the Devotee to recognize and Eliminate these Negative traits. It reinforces the Truth that the Divine resides in Clarity and Promptness. Thus, Procrastination becomes a Sin against one's own soul.
