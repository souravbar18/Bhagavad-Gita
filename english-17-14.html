Chapter 17, Shloka 14

देवद्विजगुरुप्राज्ञपूजनं शौचमार्जवम्।
ब्रह्मचर्यमहिंसा च शारीरं तप उच्यते।। १७.१४ ।।

Summary Translation:

Austerity of the body consists in worship of the Supreme Lord, the brāhmaṇas, the spiritual master, and superiors like the father and mother, and in cleanliness, simplicity, celibacy and nonviolence.

Detailed Analysis:

In this verse, Lord Krishna begins His Exposition on austerity (Tapas) by defining the Discipline of the body (Shariram Tapas). He provides a List of actions that constitute Physical austerity: worship of Devas (Supreme Lord/Demigods), Dvija (learned Brahmins), Guru (spiritual master), and Prajna (wise superiors). Added to this are Shaucham (cleanliness), Arjavam (simplicity/straightforwardness), Brahmacharyam (celibacy/restraint), and Ahimsa (non-violence). This Analysis is Profound because it shows that Austerity is not about Torture but about Respect and Purity. For a Hindu, this Shloka is a Map for Conduct. Krishna is Identifying that the Body must be Engaged in Service to be purified. The analysis reveals that Simplicity and Cleanliness are the Foundations of a Spiritual life. Krishna’s Diagnosis proves that Non-violence is a Necessity for Harmony. The Significance of this shloka is that it provides a Definition of Strength. Krishna’s Insight proves that Restraint (Brahmacharya) is Empowering. The Lesson here is that Growth requires Discipline of the Senses. This verse acts as a Standard for Tapasya. It reinforces the Truth that the Body is a Instrument for Service. Ultimately, Salvation is Supported by a Body that is Disciplined. This shloka is a Celebration of Order in Physical life.
