Chapter 16 , Shloka 22

एतैर्विमुक्तः कौन्तेय तमोद्वारैस्त्रिभिर्नरः।
आचरत्यात्मनः श्रेयस्ततो याति परां गतिम्।। १६.२२ ।।

Summary Translation:

The man who has escaped these three gates of hell, O son of Kuntī, performs acts conducive to self-realization and thus gradually attains the supreme destination.

Detailed Analysis:

In this verse, Lord Krishna describes the Reward for those who successfully close the three gates of Darkness. By being Vimuktah (liberated) from the three Tamo-dvaraih (gates of ignorance), a person gains the Clarity to pursue Atmanah-shreyas (what is beneficial for the soul). This Pursuit of welfare is the Foundation of spiritual growth. Krishna is Identifying that liberation is not a Accident but a Achievement earned through Discipline. The analysis reveals that once the Obstacles of lust, anger, and greed are removed, the Path to the Param Gatim (Supreme Destination) becomes visible. For a Hindu, this Journey involves Alignment with the Divine will. Krishna is Encouraging Arjuna by showing that Success is Possible for anyone who makes the Choice to avoid demonic traits. The Significance of this shloka is that it emphasizes Action (Acharati) over mere Belief. One must actively perform acts conducive to self-realization. This Transformation leads to Elevation. Krishna’s Diagnosis proves that the Darkness of the lower gates is the only thing standing between the soul and Perfection. The Grace of the Lord is always available, but the soul must Qualify itself by escaping the Trap of the triple vices. This verse acts as a Beacon of hope, confirming that Liberation is the Birthright of those who choose Purity. It reinforces the Truth that the Destination of a soul depends on its Conductivity to divine influence. Ultimately, Happiness and Salvation are the results of Perseverance on the path of Dharma.
