॥ Chapter 2, Shloka 51 ॥

कर्मजं बुद्धियुक्ता हि फलं त्यक्त्वा मनीषिणः ।
जन्मबन्धविनिर्मुक्ताः पदं गच्छन्त्यनामयम् ॥ २.५१ ॥

Summary Translation:

The wise, engaged in devotional service, take pride in the results of their work in the material world. In this way they become free from the cycle of birth and death and attain the state beyond all miseries.

Detailed Analysis:

Krishna reinforces the ultimate reward of 'Buddhi Yoga.' He says, 'karma-jam buddhi-yukta hi phalam tyaktva manishinah'—the wise ('Manishinah'), endowed with equanimity, give up the 'Karma-jam' (fruits born of action). By doing so, they become 'janma-bandha-vinirmuktah'—freed from the 'Bandha' (bondage) of 'Janma' (birth). Finally, they reach the 'padam gacchanty-anamayam'—the 'Anamayam' (beyond suffering or disease) 'Padam' (state/destination). This analysis focuses on the 'Causality of Liberation.' Krishna is explaining the 'Linkage' between 'Mental Renunciation' and 'Cosmic Freedom.' The reason we are born again and again is the 'Karmic Debt' created by our desire-filled actions. When a 'Manishinah' (a person of refined intellect) gives up the 'Phalam' (fruit), they stop creating new 'Debt.' Without 'Debt,' there is no reason for the 'Universe' to force a 'New Body' upon the soul. This shloka is the 'Final Warranty' of 'Karma Yoga'.

The detailed analysis of 'Anamayam' (beyond suffering) is profound. 'Amaya' literally means disease or pain. 'Anamayam' is the state where the 'Soul' is restored to its 'Original Health' of pure bliss. In our practical lives, we are always trying to fix one 'Amaya' (problem) only to find another. Krishna is teaching 'Root-Cause Resolution.' By giving up the 'Attachment' to fruits, we reach a 'State' that is unaffected by the 'Infections' of material nature. This shloka provides a 'Map to the Absolute.' It teaches that 'Liberation' is not a 'Geographic Place' (like a physical heaven), but an 'Ontological Status.' Krishna is telling Arjuna that the peace he seeks is not in the 'Forest' he wants to run to, but in the 'State' of 'Buddhi Yoga.' This verse is the ' Foundation of Eternal Freedom,' proving that 'Renunciation' is the fastest 'Vehicle' to 'Reach' the 'Self.' By using the term 'Manishinah,' Krishna is appealing to Arjuna's 'Reason.' A 'Manishinah' is not a blind believer, but a 'Clear Thinker' who sees the 'Futility' of chasing perishable fruits. This shloka is the 'Universal Promise of Sanatana Dharma,' forcing us to realize that 'Immortality' is the natural 'Residue' of a life lived without 'Egoic Claim.' This is the ' Conclusion' of the argument for 'Action,' proving that 'Right Action' is the only 'Path' that truly 'Ends all Action' (*Moksha*).

Deep Philosophical Significance and Analysis:

Philosophically, Shloka 2.51 explores the 'End of the Cycle' (*Samsara-mukti*). , 'Janma' (birth) is a 'Bandha' (bondage) because it subjects the 'Infinite Atman' to 'Finite Limitations.' This verse highlights the 'Theory of Result-Renunciation.' Philosophically, it addresses the 'Mechanism of Descent.' , the soul 'Descends' into 'Matter' due to its 'Phala-hetu' (desire for results). By 'Phalam Tyaktva' (abandoning results), the soul 'Ascends' back to its 'Padam' (source).

The framework of 'Anamayam' refers to the 'Immutable State' of the 'Absolute.' , everything in 'Prakriti' is 'S-amaya' (subject to decay). The 'Atman' is 'Anamayam.' Philosophically, this verse addresses the 'Ontology of the Wise' (*Manishinah*). , 'Wisdom' is the 'Capacity' to 'Discern' between the 'Bondage' of the 'Fruit' and the 'Freedom' of the 'Self.' This shloka is the 'Justification for Karma Yoga.' , 'Karma Yoga' is 'Sannyasa' (renunciation) in 'Action.' Philosophically, it highlights the 'Relationship between Attachment and Rebirth.' , 'Attachment' is the 'Karmic Glue.' Without it, the soul is 'Vinirmuktah' (totally free). Krishna is teaching 'Metaphysical Sovereignty.' From a religious perspective, this shloka teaches 'Final Union.' This verse is the ' Capstone of the Path of Action,' providing the logical structure to understand that 'Duty' is the 'Process' and 'Divinity' is the 'Result.' It highlights the 'Sanatana' wisdom that 'Paradise' is not 'Found' but 'Realized' through the 'Intelligence' that has stopped 'Bargaining' with 'Time'.
