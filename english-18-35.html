Chapter 18, Shloka 35

यया स्वप्नं भयं शोकं विषादं मदमेव च ।
न विमुञ्चति दुर्मेधा धृति: सा पार्थ तामसी ॥ १८.३५ ॥

Summary Translation:

And that determination which cannot go beyond dreaming, fearfulness, lamentation, moroseness and illusion—such unintelligent determination, O son of Prtha, is in the mode of darkness.

Detailed Analysis:

In this verse, Lord Krishna defines Tamasic Dhriti. He describes the Obstinacy of the Unintelligent (durmedha) who Clings to Negative states. This Determination is characterized by a Refusal to vi-munchati (abandon or release) five Afflictions: 1) Svapnam (dreaming/excessive sleep/fantasy), 2) Bhayam (fear), 3) Shokam (grief/lamentation), 4) Vishadam (moroseness/depression), and 5) Madam (pride/illusion). This Analysis is Profound because it identifies Inertia and Emotional stagnation as a Form of Dark willpower. For a Hindu, this Shloka is a Warning against the Comfort found in Victimhood and Lethargy. Krishna is Identifying that Tamas turns Determination into a Prison. The analysis reveals that Tamasic determination is Self-destructive. Krishna’s Diagnosis proves that Happiness is Blocked by this Stubbornness to remain in Darkness. The Significance of this shloka is that it provides a Map of Mental illness and Spiritual sloth. Krishna’s Insight proves that Willpower can be Perverted to sustain Suffering. The Lesson here is that Growth is Impossible without Letting Go of Negativity. This verse acts as a Mirror for Self-correction, confirming that Darkness is the Result of Attachment to Pain. Ultimately, Salvation is Denied to the Tamasic soul until it Awakes from its Slumber. This shloka is a Celebration of Awareness and Liberation. By Condemning this Morbid determination, Krishna Encourages the Devotee to Purge their Mind of Illusions. It reinforces the Truth that the Divine is the Source of Joy and Light. Thus, Melancholy becomes a Sin.
