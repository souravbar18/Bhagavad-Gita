Chapter 18, Shloka 56

सर्वकर्माण्यपि सदा कुर्वाणो मद्व्यपाश्रय: ।
मत्प्रसादादवाप्नोति शाश्वतं पदमव्ययम् ॥ १८.५६ ॥

Summary Translation:

Though engaged in all kinds of activities, My devotee, under My protection, reaches the eternal and imperishable abode by My grace.

Detailed Analysis:

In this verse, Lord Krishna gives a tremendous assurance to all householders and workers. He says that even while sarva-karmani api sada kurvanah—performing all kinds of activities constantly—one who is mad-vyapashrayah (under My protection or taking shelter in Me) achieves the ultimate goal. Through mat-prasadad (by My grace), such a person reaches the shashvatam padam avyayam—the eternal and imperishable abode. This verse is a beautiful synthesis of Karma Yoga and Bhakti Yoga. For a Hindu, it is the ultimate promise of security. The analysis reveals that the external nature of our work is less important than our internal dependence on God. Krishna is identifying that the shelter (vyapashraya) of the Lord acts as an invisible shield that prevents the reactions of work (Karma) from binding the soul. This verse serves as a source of immense hope; it proves that we do not need to be perfect or pure by our own strength. We only need to be sincere in our surrender. The term Mat-prasadad (by My grace) is the most powerful element in spiritual life. It suggests that while effort is necessary, the final breakthrough is a gift from the Divine. Krishna's diagnosis proves that the burden of work is dissolved when we work for Him and with Him. This shloka provides a standard for a life of active devotion. It encourages the individual to perform their duties in the world while keeping the mind anchored in the Supreme. It reinforces the idea that the imperishable abode is not a distant place, but a state of being that can be accessed right now through grace. Thus, the Gita teaches that the greatest freedom is found in the greatest dependence on the Divine. By following this path, the devotee turns every mundane act into a spiritual ritual. This verse is the final word on how to balance worldly responsibilities with spiritual aspirations. It proves that the Lord's protection is sufficient to overcome all obstacles. Therefore, taking shelter in the Supreme while performing one's duty is the surest way to immortality.
