Chapter 18, Shloka 6

एतान्यपि तु कर्माणि सङ्गं त्यक्त्वा फलानि च ।
कर्तव्यानीति मे पार्थ निश्चितं मतमुत्तमम् ॥ १८.६ ॥

Summary Translation:

All these activities should be performed without attachment or any expectation of result. That is My final opinion, O son of Pṛthā.

Detailed Analysis:

In this verse, Lord Krishna provides His Finality on the nature of Action. After establishing in the previous verse that sacrifice, charity, and penance are purifiers, He now Refines the Method of their execution. Krishna uses the words Etany-api tu karmani, meaning that all these activities, including the Duties of one's station in life, must be Conducted. The Requirement He sets is twofold: Sangam tyaktva (abandoning attachment) and Phalani ca (abandoning the results). This Analysis is Authoritative because Krishna labels this as His nishchitam matam uttamam—His Definitive and Supreme opinion. For a Hindu, this Shloka is the Heart of Karma Yoga. Krishna is Identifying that the Problem is not the Work itself, but the Bondage created by the Ego. The analysis reveals that Attachment acts as a Poison that Corrupts the merit of even Virtue. Krishna’s Diagnosis proves that Liberation is Possible while remaining active in the world, provided the Intention is Pure. The Significance of this shloka is that it provides a Resolution to the Conflict between Action and Renunciation. Krishna’s Insight proves that Detachment is Superior to Inaction. The Lesson here is that Growth is Guaranteed when the Individual acts as a Instrument of the Divine. This verse acts as a Mandate for Duty, confirming that Responsibility is a Path to the Absolute. Ultimately, Salvation is Attained by Transcending the Selfishness of Rewards. This shloka is a Celebration of Clarity. By Declaring His Opinion, Krishna Erases all Ambiguity for the Seeker. It reinforces the Truth that the Divine demands Sincerity over Outcomes. Thus, Performance of duty becomes a Offering.
