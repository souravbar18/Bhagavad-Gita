Chapter 17, Shloka 23

ॐ तत्सदिति निर्देशो ब्रह्मणस्त्रिविधः स्मृतः।
ब्राह्मणास्तेन वेदाश्च यज्ञाश्च विहिताः पुरा।। १७.२३ ।।

Summary Translation:

From the beginning of creation, the three words om tat sat were used to indicate the Supreme Absolute Truth. These three symbolic representations were used by brāhmaṇas while chanting the hymns of the Vedas and during sacrifices for the satisfaction of the Supreme.

Detailed Analysis:

In this verse, Lord Krishna reveals the Ultimate key to Purity: the sacred syllables Om Tat Sat. He explains that this Threefold designation (nirdesho) of Brahman has been Established since the beginning of creation (pura). This Analysis is Fundamental because it shows how Human action is Linked to the Absolute Truth. For a Hindu, Om Tat Sat is the Seal of Authenticity. Krishna is Identifying that Brahmins, Vedas, and Yajnas (sacrifices) were all Ordained through these sounds. The analysis reveals that Sound is the Foundation of Spiritual life. Krishna’s Diagnosis proves that Imperfection in any Act can be Corrected by invoking the Divine name. The Significance of this shloka is that it provides a Bridge between Practice and Perfection. Krishna’s Insight proves that Transcendence is Accessible through these symbols. The Lesson here is that Sincerity must be Rooted in the Supreme. This verse acts as a Introduction to the Conclusion of the chapter. It reinforces the Truth that all Efforts must Point toward the Ultimate Reality. Ultimately, Salvation is Facilitated by the Power of Om Tat Sat. This shloka is a Celebration of Continuity from the Dawn of time to the present Moment. By Invoking these names, the seeker Purifies their Existence.
