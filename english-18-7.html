Chapter 18, Shloka 7

नियतस्य तु संन्यासः कर्मणो नोपपद्यते ।
मोहात्तस्य परित्यागस्तामसः परिकीर्तितः ॥ १८.७ ॥

Summary Translation:

Prescribed duties should never be renounced. If one abandons his prescribed duties because of illusion, such renunciation is said to be in the mode of ignorance.

Detailed Analysis:

In this verse, Lord Krishna defines Tamasic Renunciation. He begins by stating a Principle: Niyatasya tu sannyasah karmano nopapadyate, which means that the Renunciation of Prescribed Duties is never Appropriate. Krishna explains that when such Duties are Abandoned out of Mohat (illusion or delusion), it is Categorized as Tamasic. This Analysis is Essential because it distinguishes between Wisdom and Ignorance. For a Hindu, this Shloka is a Warning against Laziness disguised as Spirituality. Krishna is Identifying that Illusion leads the soul to Neglect its Responsibilities. The analysis reveals that Tamas creates a Fog that Blinds the individual to the Importance of their Karma. Krishna’s Diagnosis proves that Avoidance of work is not Peace, but Degradation. The Significance of this shloka is that it provides a Boundary for Sannyasa. Krishna’s Insight proves that Duty is a Necessity for the Stability of the world. The Lesson here is that Growth is Thwarted when Duty is Refused out of Mental darkness. This verse acts as a Guide for Integrity, confirming that Failure is the Result of Cowardice. Ultimately, Salvation is Obstructed by Tamasic habits. This shloka is a Celebration of Diligence. By Condemning illusory renunciation, Krishna Encourages the Devotee to face their Life with Courage and Discipline. It reinforces the Truth that the Divine is found through the Fulfillment of one's Purpose. Thus, Duty becomes a Anchor.
