Chapter 17, Shloka 11

अफलाकाङ्क्षिभिर्यज्ञो विधिदृष्टो य इज्यते।
यष्टव्यमेवेति मनः समाधाय स सात्त्विकः।। १७.११ ।।

Summary Translation:

Of sacrifices, the sacrifice performed according to the directions of scripture, as a matter of duty, by those who desire no reward, is of the nature of goodness.

Detailed Analysis:

In this verse, Lord Krishna provides a Definition of sacrifice (Yajna) in the Mode of goodness (Sattva). He identifies three Criteria for a sacrifice to be considered Sattvic. First, it must be Aphalakankshibhir—performed by those who have no Desire for material rewards. Second, it must be Vidhi-drishto—conducted strictly according to Injunctions found in the scriptures. Third, it must be performed with the Conviction that yashtavyam-eveti—sacrifice is a Duty that must be done. This Analysis proves that true Worship is not a Transaction with the Divine but an act of Purifying the heart. For a Hindu, this Shloka is Critical because it distinguishes between Devotion and Commerce. Krishna is Identifying that the Mind must be Samadhaya (fixed/steady) in the performance of duty without Distraction. The analysis reveals that Sattvic sacrifice leads to Liberation because it does not create Karma. Krishna’s Diagnosis proves that Purity in worship is a Prerequisite for spiritual Advancement. The Significance of this shloka is that it provides a Standard for Rituals. By Following the scriptures without Greed, the soul Aligns itself with the Cosmic Order (Rta). Krishna’s Insight proves that Sacrifice is a Tool for Ego-dissolution. The Lesson here is that Sincerity and Discipline are the Hallmarks of goodness. This verse acts as a Guide for Life, confirming that Action performed as Duty is the Path to the Supreme. Ultimately, Salvation is Accessible to those who Worship with Detachment. This shloka is a Celebration of Integrity in Spiritual practices.
