Chapter 16 , Shloka 16

यः शास्त्रविधिमुत्सृज्य वर्तते कामकारतः।
न स सिद्धिमवाप्नोति न सुखं न परां गतिम्।। १६.२३ ।।

Summary Translation:

He who discards scriptural injunctions and acts according to his own whims attains neither perfection, nor happiness, nor the supreme destination.

Detailed Analysis:

This shloka establishes the Authority of the scriptures (Shastras) as the Compass for human life. Lord Krishna warns that anyone who Utsrijya (discards) the Shastra-vidhim (scriptural injunctions) and acts Kama-karatah (according to their own whims or lust) will face total Failure. They will Na-apnoti (not attain) Siddhim (perfection), Sukham (happiness), or Param Gatim (the supreme destination). This Analysis proves that individual Ego is a poor guide for Conduct. Scriptural rules are Designed to help the soul navigate the Dangers of the material world. For a Hindu, the Vedas and other shastras are the Manifestation of Divine Wisdom. Krishna is Identifying that acting on one’s own Whims is a demonic trait that leads to Bondage. The Significance of this shloka is that it defines the Boundary between Freedom and License. True Freedom is found in following Law. Krishna’s Diagnosis shows that Satisfaction of the senses without Discipline is a Mirage. Without Alignment with the shastras, the soul remains Groundless. This verse acts as a Foundation for the Necessity of scriptural study and obedience. It reinforces the Truth that the Path to God is Structured. Those who Reject this structure Forfeit their chance at Bliss. Ultimately, Guidance from the Lord through the scriptures is Essential for any Advancement in spiritual life. This shloka is a Call to Submission before the Wisdom of the Supreme.
